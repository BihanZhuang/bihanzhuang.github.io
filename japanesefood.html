Why I love Japanese food so much

There is a huge variety of dishes, including sushi, sashimi, tempura, hibachi, ramen, etc. Major ingredients being seafood and vegetables, Japanese food is very healthy and might be the reason behind the long Japanese life expectancy (third in the world). Japanese food can be made so delicate that it deserves to be called an art. Most importantly though, the food is SO delicious!

Fishes

On average, each person in Japan consumes around 100 grams of fish every day, in forms such as sushi, tempura and sashimi. The omega-3 fatty acids in fish are linked to heart protection and improved circulation. Most common fishes used in Japanese food are sake (salmon), maguro (tuna), unagi (freshwater eel), hamachi (yellowtail tuna), fugu (blowfish), and so on, though in the US it is hard to find fugu in common Japanese restaurants. I personally like these best when they are raw so that the distinct texture and flavor of each kind can be tasted to the full extent.

Shrimps

Shrimp (ebi) is one of the most common ingredients in Japanese cuisine and many different kinds of them are used! Kuruma-ebi is most commonly found in Japanese restaurants and they are usually served cooked in tempura or boiled over sushi. My favorite kind of shrimp is the ama-ebi, which has a slimy texture and very fresh, sweet taste that combines perfectly with sushi rice. More high-end shrimps that are served include botan-ebi (also sweet) and shiro-ebi.

Shellfishes

Shellfishes are in my opinion best when they are raw. Aka-gai (red clam), awabi (abalone), miru-gai (geoduck), there is such a great variety of shellfishes that are produced in Japan and they serve as high-quality sashimi or nigiri ingredients.

Vegetables

Japanese people use a variety of special vegetables in their daily cuisine. I am just listing a few here. Satoimo (taro root) can be added to miso soup, Japanese hot pot (nabe), Japanese curry or appear battered and deep fried. Nagaimo (yam) is usually put in soba or udon noodles, or mixed with dashi (fish stock) for flavor. Gobo (burdock root) comes in a delicious dish -- stir fried and glazed with soya sauce, sugar and sake. Okura (okra) is a summer vegetable that is often eaten raw in salads, deep fried in tempura, or served with soya sauce and katsuobushi (smoked bonito flakes).
